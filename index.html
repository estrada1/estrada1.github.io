Hello! I am currently working as a post-doc in the Biorobotics Lab at EPFL under the NCCR robotics grant.


I am passionate about designing, prototyping and validating robotic hardware for applications where the mechanics of the system are integral to functionality. Most of my projects integrate a mixture of mechanical design, electronics, software and analysis of system dynamics to achieve this.

I completed my PhD with the Biomimetics and Dexterous Manipulation Lab at Stanford University. I worked on perching, grasping and multimodal robots. My work focused on applying controllable adhesives to enable robotic systems, such as small aerial robots.

Early inspiration to go into mechanical engineering includes vague memories of bio-inspired robots on television during high school, videos of Raibert's first hopping robots from the late ’80s, undergraduate research with the Biomimetic Robotics Lab at MIT, and a broad interest as to why biological systems often beat out robots when it comes to interacting with their environment (my favorite read on this).
