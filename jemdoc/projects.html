ME2.750: Precision Machine Design (MIT Fall 2011)

 

I worked as part of a student team of five, advised by a surgeon, to develop the SutureTie, a hand-held device that used zip-tie sutures to occlude blood flow through discrete sections of the liver. Our team ultimately produced a proof-of-concept prototype which was demonstrated on an ex-vivo, porcine liver.

The project provided experience defining functional requirements, combing through prior art, working with regular input from our assigned surgeon, and able to sit in on a liver resection.
